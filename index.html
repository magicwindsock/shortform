MOVING BACKWARDS



Late 2018...

As TikTok spread like wildfire across the phones of teens and adults alike, it did not take long for short-form content to become a staple in the contemporary online world once again. Many considered TikTok to be the spiritual successor to Vine and Musical.ly, since both had declined in popularity following the former's purchase by Twitter in 2016, and the latter's technology and userbase being incoporated into TikTok in the summer of 2018. Just as the app itself saw thousands of downloads by the hour, videos published on TikTok were reposted on countless other social media platforms, instantly reshaping what Internet-users deemed "comedy" on an astronomical scale.



ANTI-VATION



In the five years that TikTok has been dominating cyberspace, it has also sparked innovation among several other social media platforms, for better or for worse. YouTube and Instagram are notable examples, having introduced their own short-form video features in 2020 and 2021. These features, known as YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels, allowed users to create and share quick digestible videos, and were both very similar to TikTok in terms of functionality.

It would not be long before algorithms adhered to this style of content on either platform, and the same could be said for the users themselves. The popularity of these features caused accounts to grow exponentially, and the creators behind them to become household names. These means of content production, however, would not be without its consequences, especially on generations renound for their fixation on technology. With children all over the world spending more time on their parents' iPads and smartphones with very little supervision, it was only a matter of time before the impact of accessible, fast-paced, short-form content took its toll. More than ever, controversial figures were able to share their messages with impressionable children across the globe, spreading a slew of misinformation, hate speech, and other harmful ideologies.



CHECKMATE



Teen and tweens, the primary demographic of these platforms, took refuge on a boat of short-form content that a new wave of creators were riding. These creators, who cultivated audiences based on factors such as their personalities, outlandish beliefs, or wealth, saw exponential growth amongst the three aforementioned social platforms. Colorful and flashy thumbnails, clickbait titles, and a myriad of other tactics conviced their young followings to come back for more, only bolstering support for their questionable agendas. This genre of content, known among its dissenters as "content farming" or "brainrot", would receive an even stronger innovation in 2023, when AI generated media began infiltrating the algorithms. Misinformation now laid in the palms of millions, who depended on fake people, fake voices, and fake news for entertainment. Some people are not aware that they have fallen into such dark rabbit holes. Some never come out.



NEVER ENOUGH

I was taught that the world was a terrible place, and that I should be afraid of it.
